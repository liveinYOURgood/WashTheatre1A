A Brief History...

The Washington Theatres was opened in 1926. It was built for both Silent Movies and Vaudeville. The theatres seated 800. It was proclaimed "The Showplace of Northwestern New Jersey." It was equipped with a Robert Morton 3 manual 2 ranks organ. Four years later it was modernized and wired with the addition of sound. Opened by the Lions Theatres Circuit, the Washington Theatres has had many owners.

In the 1970's the theatres was twinned. At this time much of its ornate style was covered over or destroyed. Over the years the Washington Theatres fell into a state of disrepair, and closed in 1997. It did not die however. It was briefly reopened by a local community group.

In 1998 the Washington Theatres was acquired by Galaxy Theatres. Extensive renovations were done to restore the lobby area back to its grandeur. Also extensive cleanups and repairs to the auditoriums was done. However, the Washington Theatres did not survive long again in operation, closing about 2001. It was reopened in May 2006 but had closed by 2016. (Most copy here from internet. Some older pictures on this website where the theatres were in business also from internet as well.)
